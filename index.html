Wormholes are speculative structures that link faraway points in spacetime. Imagine that you are travelling through the cosmos, with the intention of heading towards a distant planet across the universe. However, your estimated arrival time would be lightyears away — a frustrating and tiresome journey, if you could even live that long. Wormholes can theoretically shorten your journey almost instantly, but you would miss the sights of the strange but beautiful planets that the vast galaxies have to offer.

The story of Folding Beijing draws parallels to the concept of the wormhole, in which the protagonist experiences a transcendence from a world of poverty to a life of luxury within a day — a journey that would have otherwise taken a portion of a lifetime.

Now, you as a reader can choose to read this story, accompanied by tales from Invisible Planets, according to whichever you see as an important factor while travelling:

Experiencing the journey as it is

OPTION 2:
Getting to the destination as fast as possible
